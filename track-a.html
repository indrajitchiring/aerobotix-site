Track A Syllabus

How Junior Robotics Progresses

This pathway follows the early Sparklebots foundation sequence so students move from hands-on mechanics into visual coding before stepping up to more advanced robotics tracks later.

Course 01

RoboCHAMP

The foundation course for young learners. Students explore simple machines, structural assembly, gears, pulleys, balance, and mechanical creativity through a non-programmable robotics kit. This is where engineering intuition begins.

Classes 4-6 24 Hours No Coding
Course 02

Scratch Programming

Students then learn visual block coding through animations, stories, and games. It introduces loops, events, variables, conditions, and logical problem-solving before they ever touch text-based programming.

Classes 4-7 24 Hours MIT Scratch
Course 03

Advanced RoboCHAMP

After basic RoboCHAMP, students move into motorized mechanical systems. They begin building more complex motion-driven robots, learn drive mechanisms, and take on teamwork and design challenges that bridge them toward programmable robotics.

Lite + Full 20-36 Hours Motorized Builds
Next Stage

Ready for Track B Progression

Once students are confident with RoboCHAMP and Scratch-style logic, they are ready to move into the next pathway where programmable robotics, electronics, sensors, mobile control, and more advanced robot builds begin in earnest.

Mechanical Thinking Logic Ready Bridge to Robotics
Projects & Models

What Students Build in Track A

The junior path is highly hands-on. Students continuously build named mechanical models and develop an intuitive feel for structure, movement, balance, and creative engineering.

Mechanical Builds

Skill Outcomes

What Junior Learners Gain

  • Understanding of gears, levers, pulleys, wheels, axles and linkages.
  • Confidence in assembling structures and troubleshooting model stability.
  • Visual coding logic through Scratch-based stories, loops, conditions and events.
